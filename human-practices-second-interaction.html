2nd interaction

Presentation to teacher's university, focus groups, 2nd farm investigation

Focus groups

We carry out Focus Groups to exhibit the solution design development by the team and receive feedback from the experts, farmers and exporters.

Evidence and informed consent

  • First focus group. Monday morning
      • Investigation
      • Video
  • Second focus group. Monday evening
      • Investigation
      • Video
  • Third focus group. Wednesday evening
      • Investigation
      • Video
  • Fourth focus group. Thursday evening
      • Investigation
      • Video

    • Hypothesis to validate:

    The solutions proposed by the laboratory area are suitable to solve some of the main problems of the stakeholders involved.

    Objectives

    To obtain the opinion of the producers and/or marketers of berries on the five proposals of solution developed by the team.

  • Determine the level of interest generated by the problems presented.
  • Determine the viability of the solution proposals.
  • Determine the 2 most interesting solution proposals.
  • Determine the least interesting solution proposal.

    • Methodology

    The area of Laboratories and Genetic Engineering developed infographics that explained 4 different solutions to the most common problems identified in the 1st Interaction. The proposed solutions were:

  • Thermo-protective proteins
  • Frog foam for water retention in soils
  • Biosensors for fungal pest detection

    • A registration form was created for multiple times, the form presented the following questions:

    Section 01: Presentation.

    a. Presentation and objectives of the interaction.

    b. Contact information (e-mail).

    c. Focus Group information.

    Section 02: Contact information.

    a. Name.

    b. Role (producer/farmer, marketer, or both).

    Section 03: Informed Consent

    a) The Informed Consent Letter was presented and requested to be read and accepted

    The following video invitation was made and posted on the team’s social networks

    Through a presentation the dynamic was as follows:

  • Presentation of the team, our target and the moderators.
  • 2. Letter of Informed Consent once accepted the recording began.
  • 3. Presentation by producers or marketers.
  • 4. First round of questions (Questions 1 - 5).
      4.1 What do you consider the most serious problems affecting berry farming?
      4.2 What are some useful solutions?
      4.3 If you could create a solution to all your problems, what would be the most important aspects?
      4.4 What needs should the solution meet?
      4.5 What is more important to you? It distributes from 1 - 10 points of importance to the following problems: Water (Pollution and/or scarcity), pests (Insects or fungi) and climate (Changes in temperature and extreme climate).
  • 5. Presentation of infographics.
  • 6. Second round of questions (Questions 6 - 9).
  • 6.1 Do you think the solutions presented are feasible? why?
  • 6.2 Which of these solutions would you use or catch your eye? Does it solve something you need?
  • 6.3 What is the most unnecessary, least feasible proposal? Which would you not use?
  • 6.4 If you could put some of the above solutions together, to cover more problems in one, what would they be?
